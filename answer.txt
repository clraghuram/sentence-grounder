Regarding considerations when initiating Mounjaro in patients taking insulin, according to Section 4.5 of the Summary of Product Characteristics, Mounjaro lowers blood glucose. When initiating Mounjaro, consider reducing the dose of concomitantly administered insulin to reduce the risk of hypoglycaemia.

Concerning insulin dose adjustment recommendations, Section 4.2 of the Summary of Product Characteristics states that when tirzepatide is added to existing therapy of insulin, a reduction in the dose of insulin may be considered to reduce the risk of hypoglycaemia. Blood glucose self-monitoring is necessary to adjust the dose of insulin, and a stepwise approach to insulin reduction is recommended.

As for precautions and monitoring requirements, according to Section 4.4 of the Summary of Product Characteristics, patients receiving tirzepatide in combination with insulin may have an increased risk of hypoglycaemia, and this risk may be lowered by a reduction in the dose of insulin. Additionally, Section 4.8 notes that clinically significant hypoglycaemia, defined as blood glucose less than 3.0 mmol per litre, or severe hypoglycaemia requiring the assistance of another person, occurred in 14 to 19 percent of patients, at a rate of 0.43 to 0.64 events per patient year, when tirzepatide was added to basal insulin. In the SURPASS-5 study, patients with inadequate glycaemic control using insulin glargine with or without metformin were treated with tirzepatide, and the median dose of insulin glargine at week 40 was 29 units per day for the tirzepatide 15 mg group compared to 59 units per day for placebo, while the baseline estimated median dose was 34 units per day. Section 4.7 also notes that when tirzepatide is used in combination with insulin, patients should be advised to take precautions to avoid hypoglycaemia while driving and using machines.
